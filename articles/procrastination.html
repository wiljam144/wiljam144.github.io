Introduction

This article series was originally written as a single article, that I decided to write because I wanted to challenge myself with writing something that non-technical people could understand, then I decided to split it into two parts. The one you're reading right now, about procrastination and how to stop it. And a second part covers more philosophical topics and how to improve quality of your life that I thought about because of writing the first part (the second part is not yet published since I have a lot of exams in the coming week so you'll need to wait a little). This whole chain of events that was triggered by quote of my maths professor: "When you come back home, insead of getting on social media, lay in your bed for 15 minutes, then you will see how boring procrastinating and doing nothing really is, that's what you're doing when you're scrolling through social media, but social media hide the boredom and wasting of time". I decided to give it a try for fun. After the 15 minutes I got bored so badly that it hurt me psychically. Doing nothing is painful. This "experiment" showed me really effectively that there are better things to do then endlessly scrolling through social media.

How I Stopped Procrastinating

This expierence showed me that procrastinating was killing my productivity and taking away my free time, it didn't offer a solution, but it gave me a hint. I did kind of self-diagnosis of what was causing the procrastination. Now (suprise) it was social media and (surprise) computer games, yes I know I sound like a really old person, but hear me out. Instead of doing my work I did something so unproductive like scrolling through social media. Now let's do a simple analysis. What I get from watching TikTok for 30 minutes? For me it's nothing, no new knowledge, no new ideas, just a wasted 30 minutes and unfinished work. While there's nothing wrong with watching a couple of videos or playing a game when your work is done and dusted and you want to relax. You really shouldn't do something when there's unfinished work. I solved the issue of scrolling instead of working in a really extreme way, I uninstalled all social media apart from reddit and instagram, reddit because I don't really scroll through reddit at home and instagram because my school council posts annoucements on it's instagram account so I couldn't just uninstall it. But I put a 10-minute maximal time spent on it, just enough to read the annoucements. That solved the procrastination for me, I did the work first, then scrolled through social media's front pages.

Constant Improvements

I noticed that getting rid of social media in such a way improved my well-being and life satisfaction. I am now going to sleep happy that I finished my work and every day is productive. But I thought, if doing work first boosted my productivity and satisfaction that much. What would happen if I stopped playing video games and stopped using social media completely. Let's just say it was a great idea. I stopped lifting the time limit on instagram after finishing my assignments, and uninstalled reddit and twitter so that I wouldn't use it to kill time during the commute to school. Instead I installed an RSS reader on my phone to read news and posts from my favourite blogs about topics that matter to me or are a subject of my interest like maths, computer science, cybersecurity and physics, I also added some philosophy blogs to the mix (I will write an article or post about my RSS feeds someday). So when using public transport I was learning something new every commute. But not everyday your RSS feeds are full and my commute is half an hour, because of that I found myself in situations where I would just stare out of the window of the tram because there was nothing to read. Before that whole "experiment" I was doing some trading on the stock market, but I couldn't find time to really read and follow all of the news about the market and the companies that I was planning to invest in. That meant my trading was pretty suboptimal and I couldn't gain much money from it. Well, the time found itself, the commute is the perfect place for reading such news and thinking about what stocks should I buy or sell. It worked, In about a month of trading I made profits larger than I ever did, so I bought myself a new monitor. That monitor now sort of a testament of how much I could do if spend my time correctly. When I finished my assignements, I couldn't scroll through social media or play video games, because I uninstalled them. So I needed a new thing to kill boredom. So I started writing this blog and programming side projects. These things are definitely better to do than scrolling through your twitter or instagram page, or playing League of Legends that is a paradox in it of itself (I will also write something about not enjoying a video game and still playing it). Programming side projects gives me something because it allows me to learn some technical abilities that school doesn't teach. And writing a blog is just fun in general, finishing an article gives a lot of satisfaction and seeing even the tiniest number of visitors on each article gives you a spark of joy everytime.

Some Productivity Tips I Picked Up Along the Way

First of all, mute your phone when doing work, Let's say some job takes you 2 hours to complete, when you are being constantly interrupted and distracted by a notification every 10 minutes, you will spend 3 or more hours to complete it. If you mute your phone and respond to notifications after doing the job it will take you 2 hours and 10 minutes. I'm doing a lot of competetive programming and theoretical computer science, getting distracted is the worst thing I could do because it prevents me from getting into the "zone", apart from that it causes me to forget what I was thinking about this comic presents it perfectly. Second thing is to set a clear goal for yourself, let's say "I'm gonna read 10 pages of the text book", it will motivate you, because you are getting to a set goal instead of following in the fog. You should also listen to the right music for the job, for example when doing maths the last thing I want to listen is rock that I'm listening to on the commute to the school I'd rather be listening to this playlist. That contains calm movie soundtracks that help focus and get into the "zone". Last but not least remember to take a break everytime, you're stuck on something. Our brains aren't made for sustained load. When you're unable to solve an issue, thinking about it for an hour more without a break isn't going to help you solve the problem. Take a break, for me I write one paragraph for the blog and make myself a cup of tea. Then when I come back to the issue the solution just comes to my mind instantly.

Conclusions

Stopping procrastination at it's core by removing social media and video games from the equation, allowed me to spend my time better and create a lot of healthy habits. Now I'm doing more in a day than I ever did. It also caused me to rethink how I use technology and go about my life that resulted in creating a tiny personal life philosophy, about using technology and a new way of thinking about life, but about that in the second part. I hope my tips and story will help you get over procrastination.
